This is a calculated strategic maneuver to neuter the Executive branch and consolidate absolute power in a legislative majority. By weaponizing  Article I, Section 8, Clause 18 —the Necessary and Proper Clause—Raskin and his allies seek to transform Congress into a "dominant force" that dictates the minutiae of executive and judicial operations, effectively reducing the President to a subordinate clerk.Raskin’s vision relies on a hyper-narrow, radical "textualism" intended to strip the Presidency of its historical weight. He argues the President is merely the "commander-in-chief of the army and navy" during "actual service," rather than the leader of the nation. This interpretation is a direct strike against the Unitary Executive, seeking to liquidate the President's authority over national security and economic policy. By distorting the  Take Care Clause , progressives aim to prevent the President from executing the laws faithfully, instead demanding he act as a passive conduit for congressional spending and mandates.

2. The Judicial Purge: Regulating the Last Bastion of the Republic
Having targeted the Executive, the Jacobin movement has turned its sights on the Supreme Court, the final obstacle to their radical restructuring. Utilizing the rhetoric of "judicial aggrandizement," academic architects like Professor Nicholas Bowie and pundits like Dahlia Lithwick frame judicial independence as an "anti-democratic" threat. They characterize the current Court as a "6-3 MAGA installed juggernaut," providing the pretext for a judicial purge. Their strategy is one of "Divide and Conquer": using "career professionals" within the Department of Justice and lower courts to isolate and de-legitimize the Supreme Court until it is a mere subsidiary of the legislative will.
Three Pillars of the Progressive Judicial Overhaul
1. The "Regulate and Restrict" Strategy:  Implementing ethics codes as political weapons to delegitimize conservative justices, using administrative "guardrails" to force recusal or removal and create a cowed judiciary.
2. The "Jurisdiction Stripping" Maneuver:  Advocating for Congress to use its "necessary and proper" authority to prevent the Court from reviewing progressive laws, such as the Voting Rights Act, thereby ensuring radical legislation is immune to constitutional scrutiny.
3. The "Shadow Docket" De-legitimization Campaign:  Framing the Court's essential emergency orders—often required to halt lawless executive actions—as "invisible ink" or "weasly" rulings to justify ignoring judicial stays that impede their agenda.
