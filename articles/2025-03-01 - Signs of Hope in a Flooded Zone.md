If you subscribe to *Democracy Docket*, chances are you are an avid consumer of news. Yet, even for those of us who diligently pay attention, it can be difficult to stay up to date on Donald Trump’s latest attacks on democracy.

Part of this is a failure of the legacy media itself—much of which is now more accurately described as oligarch-controlled media. However, it is also part of a deliberate Republican strategy to spread lies and disinformation about what is happening.

This is often referred to as the “flood the zone” strategy. It’s worth revisiting exactly what Steve Bannon said when he coined the phrase in 2018: *The Democrats don’t matter. The real opposition is the media. And the way to deal with them is to flood the zone with shit.*

I started *Democracy Docket* —and this newsletter in particular—to combat exactly this strategy. Each Saturday, I highlight the most important news of the week that may have been buried under the flood of misinformation the GOP has intentionally released.

This week is a prime example of why this effort is so necessary. On the one hand, the GOP wants to portray Trump as an all-powerful president imposing his will on a fearful and dispirited government. It is true that he is taking outrageous actions. It is also true that Republicans in Congress have been intimidated into serving as his rubber stamp.

However, it is not true that he is having his way. The reality is that government workers are standing up. And he is losing most of the lawsuits challenging his illegal actions. Plaintiffs, their lawyers and federal judges are not backing down. Instead, they are upholding the rule of law. It’s not a perfect system, but the news from the courts is more good than bad.

Another significant development is worth noting: the GOP’s election subversion war machine is revving up—more emboldened than ever. But it, too, is losing in court. Just this week, two different courts—one a state trial court, the other a federal appeals court—handed Republicans and their allies stinging defeats.

I’m not saying all the news is good or that democracy isn’t under immense strain. But I am saying there are signs of hope, and we must embrace them.
