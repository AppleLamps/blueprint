On Tuesday night, Donald Trump [lied](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ecmky5jtYiI) his way through the State of the Union, ordering Senate Majority Leader John Thune to go beyond the already disastrous SAVE America Act by requiring proof of citizenship to vote and banning no-excuse mail-in voting.

On Friday, I told you this would be a calamity for our elections and warned that Trump wasn't asking Congress to make these changes — he was demanding them. Now, Trump is taking matters into his own hands.

According to reports, [anti-voting activists](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/white-house-circulating-blatantly-illegal-draft-emergency-order-to-take-control-of-elections/) are working with the White House to issue an emergency executive order that would give Trump unprecedented control over our elections. The order is based on a false conspiracy theory that China interfered in the 2020 election.

Yes, it is illegal and unconstitutional, and yes, the courts would block it. But that does not mean the mere issuance of such an [order](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/read-the-laughable-legal-memo-behind-the-claim-that-trump-can-declare-a-national-voting-emergency/) would be harmless. It would cause real damage.

Trump is ready and willing to take drastic measures to swing the midterms in his favor. Every day that passes, his approval ratings sink further. His policies are growing more unpopular. The calls to release the Epstein files are growing louder.

On top of this, the DOJ is filing [five new lawsuits](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/trump-doj-expands-voter-roll-crusade-sues-five-more-states/) to obtain access to sensitive voter data. The new lawsuits — targeting Utah, Oklahoma, Kentucky, West Virginia, and New Jersey — bring the total number of states sued to 30.

Speaker Mike Johnson said it himself: "If we lose the midterms, heaven forbid, it would be the end of the Trump presidency in a real effect."

## What I'm watching in the courts

In Fulton County, two former DOJ officials submitted a court filing [accusing](https://www.democracydocket.com/news-alerts/ex-justice-department-officials-fbi-fulton-county-2020-elections-raid/) the department of failing "to live up to the high standard set by previous administrations" in its seizure of 2020 election records. The officials — former acting U.S. attorneys general Peter Keisler and Stuart Gerson — went further, condemning the department for abandoning its role as "a protector of constitutional rights and liberties."

However, ahead of an upcoming evidentiary hearing, federal Judge Jean-Paul Boulee reversed course, directing the DOJ and Fulton County to reach an agreement about the seized records outside of court before he considers additional evidence. Boulee also suggested that the DOJ may be permitted to keep the ballots it seized, regardless of the outcome in the case.

With the 2026 midterms right around the corner, that would be a terrible outcome — one that would allow Pam Bondi's DOJ to seize election records without fear of consequence.

And while the Supreme Court may eventually gut what remains of the Voting Rights Act in Louisiana v. Callais, it looks increasingly unlikely it will do so in time to affect the 2026 elections.

Still, we cannot afford to rest easy. We must continue to fight back against attacks on our democracy — but I believe we are poised to win.
