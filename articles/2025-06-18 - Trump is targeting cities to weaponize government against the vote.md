Trump is targeting cities to weaponize government against the vote6:23Donald Trump’s attack on free and fair elections took a dark turn this week. On Sunday night, Trump posted on social media that he wanted stepped-up immigration operations “in America’s largest Cities, such as Los Angeles, Chicago, and New York [because] These, and other such Cities, are the core of the Democrat Power Center, where they use Illegal Aliens to expand their Voter Base [and] cheat in Elections.”

Let’s be clear: Trump isn’t targeting these cities because of immigration. He is looking to cripple them as a source of the opposition party’s political power. That is why he called them a “power center,” and it is why he connects them to elections. Trump is laying the groundwork to misuse the federal government to undermine free and fair elections.

Trump repeated his intention again on Monday — while standing with world leaders at the G-7 summit in Canada: “Biden allowed 21 million people to come into our country… Most of those people are in the cities. All blue cities, all Democrat-run cities. And they think they are going to use them to vote. Not going to happen.”

Setting aside his bogus claims, Trump once again makes clear that his main concern is not immigration but rather electoral power. Whether you take Trump literally or seriously, the threat is the same: he is planning to use the force of the federal government to diminish Democratic power and limit its electoral success.

If this were simply another instance of Donald Trump lying about noncitizen voting, I would be less concerned. After all, Trump has been obsessively lying about noncitizen voting for as long as he has run for office. Following the 2016 election, he falsely claimed that if you deducted all the noncitizen votes, he had won the popular vote. He lost that election by 2 million votes.

After his defeat in 2020, Trump enlisted others — including his personal lawyer — to spread lies. In Arizona, Rudy Giuliani testified in a makeshift hearing on the day the election was certified that “a few hundred thousand” noncitizens voted. In the days and weeks that followed, Giuliani pegged the number at “way more than 10,000;” a “bare minimum is 40 or 50,000;” and “probably about 250,000.”

Ultimately, Giuliani was suspended from practicing law in New York based on these lies and other false claims about election fraud. While it is remarkable that the humiliation of 2020 did not stop Trump or his allies from continuing to spread the Big Lie, those lies alone pose little threat to future elections.

What makes this far more dangerous is the use of those lies to justify abuse of governmental power. We saw Trump attempt that after the 2020 election, but his team was too disorganized, and enough institutional guardrails existed within his administration to prevent him from succeeding.

We will have no such luck in 2026. Trump has already purged his top advisors of anyone willing to stand up to him and invoke the rule of law or Constitution to limit his actions. His Attorney General is an election denier who will execute whatever plan he and the White House concoct.

With control of Congress in the balance, Trump has already sought to tip the scales in his favor ahead of 2026. His executive order attempting to dictate the rules of elections was fortunately blocked by a court. But his Department of Justice is currently serving as a wingman to the GOP’s voter suppression machine.

Now, even as the battle for free and fair elections plays out in court, Trump is opening a new front. By deploying large numbers of federal agents—as well as members of the military—in major U.S. cities, Trump is setting the stage for the kind of voter suppression and election subversion we typically see in other countries.

This does not mean we will not have elections in 2026. We will. Trump does not have the power to cancel elections, and our system of hyper-local election administration is a barrier to a hostile federal takeover. That does not guarantee, however, that the elections will be entirely free and fair.

In a closely divided electorate, the intimidation or disenfranchisement of even a few thousand voters can spell the difference between winning and losing. But the misuse of government resources to target the political opposition’s “power center” can do far more damage.

Noncitizens cannot vote in federal elections. There are multiple safeguards in place — starting with voter registration — to prevent noncitizens from voting, whether intentionally or by accident. All available evidence shows these measures work.

Yes, Democrats enjoy substantial political and electoral support in major cities, just as Republicans tend to enjoy similar support in more rural areas. There is nothing suspicious about a party having geographical centers of political power. What is wrong — morally, legally and constitutionally — is the federal government targeting that power through the misuse of its authority.

That is exactly what Trump says he is doing. It is what he will continue to do unless he is forced to stop.

Lawyers like me are part of the answer. I am committed to fighting Trump, his administration, and his allies in court wherever they seek to suppress the vote or subvert the results of free and fair elections.

But lawyers cannot do this alone. The public and its elected officials must stand up strongly against Trump and his illegal tactics. We have already seen some Democratic governors do just that. But we need more.

I know there is a lot going on right now. We are constantly being asked to care about one threat or another facing our country and our democracy. But without free and fair elections, none of the other threats will matter. If Trump can deploy federal resources — including the military — to influence elections, and if he succeeds, there will be no guardrails left at all.

We must make protecting elections our top priority. By taking a stand, speaking out, and supporting pro-democracy candidates and institutions, we can defeat Trump’s sinister efforts. But only if we act together and act now.
