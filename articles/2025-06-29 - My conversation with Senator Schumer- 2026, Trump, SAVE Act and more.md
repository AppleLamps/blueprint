My conversation with Senator Schumer: 2026, Trump, SAVE Act and more20:59*This week, I sat down with Senate Democratic Leader Chuck Schumer for an interview. We talked about the Supreme Court’s decision on nationwide injunctions, how Trump’s ‘big, ugly betrayal’ bill will harm Americans, Schumer’s first meeting with Trump as president and much more.*

Marc: Senator Schumer, welcome to Defending Democracy.

Senator Schumer: Marc. Great to be here with one of the great lawyers in our country who has protected democracy in many ways and is fighting to do it at this moment, I am sure.

Marc: I appreciate that. Speaking of things that we are currently doing — Republicans seem intent on passing what appears to be an absolutely terrible bill. I’m not a policy expert, but my sense is they're trying to take away people's health care and, at the same time, give tax breaks to billionaires. What's going on?

Senator Schumer: Well, first, this is one of the worst bills that's happened in the last 100 years, and you summed it up correctly. If you had to say it in four words, you'd say: Billionaires win, families lose. This bill is just outrageous. It basically says that we're going to dramatically cut health care more than we've ever cut it before. 16 million people will lose their health care benefits. Many more who don't, who aren't on ACA or on Medicaid will have their premiums.

Even if you don't have Medicare or Medicaid, your premiums would go dramatically up because of what they've done. The cuts would decimate hospitals — we estimate one out of every three rural hospitals would close. Those are in Republican areas, most of them, and for many counties, we have counties like this in upstate New York, it's the largest employer and the only source of health care. But even big hospitals would close.

The head of one of the biggest hospitals in the Bronx told me that they could close and over a million people will lose their jobs. Often, these are jobs that are ladders up. We see it when we go to hospitals. They get paid decent wages, oftentimes, because it's union labor, and they lose those jobs. It is just devastating across the board.

But it's not the only thing they're doing. They're cutting food stamps. They're going to take food out of the mouths of hungry children. And in the bill, you'd probably get about $5 a day to feed a kid. Well, $5 doesn't buy a dozen eggs these days, with the prices going up in terms of clean energy, a bill that we passed two years ago, which was going to move America into an era of wind and solar and clean energy.

They're cutting all that back because the fossil fuel industry hates clean energy, because they know it's the future — solar panels are the cheapest way to get new energy, the quickest. The oil and gas [industries] know that they're the past, and they want to kill this, and Republicans did now.

Why are they doing all these bad things and many more — cutting education, cutting housing, cutting so much? I'll tell you why: To give tax breaks to billionaires. There's a small, very rich, greedy group of billionaires, and they just don't want to pay taxes. They hate the government in general, because the government puts barriers. [The government] protects customers, workers, the land, air and water that we treasure, and they want to cut all of that out so that they can pay almost no taxes and have no barriers in their way.

So basically, they're cutting your health care, cutting food from hungry children, cutting clean energy, which employs 840,000 people who would lose their jobs under that, all to give tax breaks for billionaires.

And one other thing more: the American people have begun to learn how bad this bill is. Donald Trump calls it his “big, beautiful bill.” We call it his big, ugly betrayal. He's betrayed the American people. The things he promised in the campaign, he's going back on and reversing. And now about 80% of the public knows what this bill is, and two thirds of them don't like it. Almost every Democrat doesn't like it, independents don't like it much either, and even a good chunk of Republicans don't like it. So that's why the Republicans are squirming.

Why is it taking them so long to put together a bill? They know that this bill is highly unpopular and will do devastation to their states. So they are squirming and delaying.

Marc: I have one question that I don't understand. I assume Medicaid cuts disproportionately hit a number of red states. You'd think the Republicans would look at this and be like, “This is terrible for our constituents. This is hurting us.” Do they know that this is bad for them, and they don't care? Are they just sucking up to Donald Trump? What is the rationale when you hear Republican senators talk about this to their constituents?

Senator Schumer: It's a great question. Lisa Murkowski summed it up best. She said, “We are all afraid.” She was the only honest one in the whole bunch. They are afraid. What are they afraid of? Retribution, vicious, nasty retribution by Donald Trump.

Here's what's been reported about Joni Ernst. It's a horrifying story. Joni Ernst was a farm girl from Iowa. She enlisted in the military. She was brutally raped. The military didn't do anything to go after the people who did it to her. So she ran for the Senate, and one of the things she ran on and wanted to change was how the military treats women who were abused. She won and to her credit, she did fix up some of the stuff in the military.

Well, Hegseth is nominated. He’s been accused of rape multiple times and of course, it won't be prosecuted under a Trump Justice Department that doesn't care about rule of law. All they care about is backing up their friends who back up Donald Trump. And so she said she couldn't vote for this guy given her history.

Joni Ernst felt so pressured by Donald Trump and Elon Musk to vote yes that she did, because they're all afraid. They're just afraid of Donald Trump.

Here's the thing they don't realize. Abe Lincoln said it best: Public sentiment is everything. Even though they're going along with Donald Trump, they're not going along with the public. So they're going to get hurt electorally. In 2017 they passed this big tax cut, we beat them on health care, and they lost seats in the House in 2018 [as a result]. Being afraid of Donald Trump may solve your short term fears, but the long term damage that you'll do with your constituents could be far more harmful to them, but [Republicans] don't quite get it yet.

Marc: People who follow democracy issues have been actually quite happy with Democrats in the Senate. You managed to kill the provision that started in the House as an effort to strip courts of the ability to hold contempt. When it came to the Senate, Republicans wanted to make it so that in order to get injunctions against the government, there had to be these big cash bonds, which would have made it impossible to sue. All of that got stripped out, or seems to have gotten stripped out by the parliamentarian after Senate Democrats made a big fuss over it. So that was a big win for you guys.

Also, I hear you’ve killed the SAVE Act, which would make it harder for people to register to vote, particularly for married women. How confident are you that you can keep that law from going forward?

Senator Schumer: I think we're confident. For this reconciliation bill, they only need 51 so even without us, they can really screw the American people. But when they need the 60, we're the barrier. They will never pass the SAVE Act in the Senate, as long as I am leader, and I believe that every Democrat will vote against it. They would need seven to add to their 53 to get 60. They won't get it.

The SAVE ACT is Jim Crow for all of America. They say you need voter ID. Well, that sounds okay. People who know the history know that the South has used voter ID to stop, particularly black people, poor people, from voting. But okay, voter ID.

But here's the voter ID you need. You need either your passport or your up to date birth certificate. So if a woman's original birth certificate said Shirley Jones, but she got married now and she's Shirley Smith, she has to find her original birth certificate. That's not just true for women who get married. It's estimated that millions and millions of people would lose their franchise.

If you read some of the people who study why democracies are lost, they chip away at democracy so that, even though there might be voting, it's not a real democracy. This is very dangerous, but we will beat the SAVE Act.

It's horrible what you said about the courts. Even today's court case, which stopped nationwide injunctions, they want pure executive power over anyone who opposes them in any way. You can look at the law firms or the universities or the nonprofit groups - [Trump and Republicans] want to destroy them, because they want to have their way. When Trump said he wanted to be a dictator, people should know he wasn't kidding. That's who he is.

Marc: One of the reasons why I've enjoyed working with you over the course of the last few decades is that you are one of the smartest, if not the smartest, political strategists Democrats have. You also fight hard—

Senator Schumer: I’m from Brooklyn, you know we fight hard!

Marc: Look what just happened in North Carolina with this state Supreme Court election that Republicans tried to steal. They tried to steal it by basically disenfranchising those military and overseas voters. And it was in part because of the law that you pioneered, you championed, you sponsored, and you brought forward in the past that we were able, in court, to beat back the efforts of Republicans to steal elections.

Senator Schumer: These people are overseas, risking their lives for us, and they're taking away their right to vote. Just because they're overseas and it takes a little longer for them to pre-register or get their mail back, it was outrageous! So we stopped that and guaranteed our soldiers and people overseas the right to vote. You know, we're fighting.

Marc: I had a front row seat to see just how hard you fought for the John R Lewis bill and the Freedom to Vote Act. You had your entire caucus supporting it. Not a single Republican in the House or Senate voted for the Freedom to Vote Act or its predecessor, the For the People Act. Why is that? When you talk to Republican colleagues, really none of them support voting rights?

Senator Schumer: It's a wicked devolution, so to speak. Ronald Reagan supported the Voting Rights Act, and it was renewed. George H. W. Bush supported the Voting Rights Act, and it was renewed. George W. Bush supported the Voting Rights Act, and it was approved again.

What happened in 2010 was that a group of hard right people started taking over the Republican Party. The Tea Party people started going against voting. You know why? They lose when everyone votes, and they hate that. When Donald Trump came in, he made it much, much worse.

Now not only are they not supporting voting rights, they're taking away voting rights left right and center. It goes along with what I said before – they don't want democracy. The Republican Party has gone down the drain and just cut off voting rights. It’s just a new Republican Party. It's nasty. It's anti-democratic. It's for a small band of billionaires. And it's really hurting democracy in America.

Marc: You are obviously a famous New Yorker. Trump is a famous New Yorker. You undoubtedly watched him over the years that he was tabloid fodder, and then through his various marriages and iterations. The line I always like about Donald Trump was something from the editors of Spy Magazine from the late 80s – it said that he was a short-fingered vulgarian. You've seen him in a lot of different settings — what makes him tick? Is he just an authoritarian? Does he believe in anything?

Senator Schumer: It's total ego. “Me, me, me.” Some people say he's an infantile narcissist. We know he's a narcissist, but the infantile part means whatever is in front of him at the moment, he goes for it, and it doesn't matter what will happen six hours after that, or what he said six hours before it.

So he'll just totally lie, totally reverse himself. He doesn't care. He does whatever he thinks benefits him at the moment. And it's dangerous, it's disastrous, it's reckless. It's what's caused our prices to go up. It's caused so many people already to lose jobs. It's caused something that'll probably be a recession if we pass this bill, because over 2 million people, about 800,000 in clean energy, over 1 million in health care, will lose their jobs. You can't lose 2 million jobs without it damaging the economy. He just sees whatever's in front of his face, and he listens to the last person who talks to him, and he makes things up.

I remember the first time I met him after he had been elected president. He had a reception for McConnell, McCarthy, Pelosi — the four leaders.

He talks about how it's so horrible how Democrats are depriving people of voting. He says, “I have a friend. He's a golfer. I think he was German. He stood on line to vote in Florida, and they took four Hispanics and moved them ahead of him.” Okay, well, the press did some research. They found out who this golfer was. His name was Bernard Langer. He's German. He never voted. He was never on line voting. He just makes things up.

I've disagreed with a lot of presidents in so many different ways, but I've never seen a president who just thinks he can lie at will and get away with it. We can't let him get away with it. That's what we're trying to do here with the Democratic majority and minority in the Senate.

Marc: On issues of democracy, I think the Senate Democrats are doing a really good job and having a fair amount of success. Being able to block these efforts to restrict voting rights is going to be among the most important things we can do for 2026…

Senator Schumer: Yeah. In 2021 and 2022, when we passed the CHIPS Act, the infrastructure bill, guns rights, a law to protect gay marriage, w had the IRA, which did more for the environment.

The next two years, when we lost the house, even though we kept the Senate, we put in 235 good, progressive judges. You helped us, in a lot of ways, find some good people. Two thirds were women, two thirds were people of color. They're our first line of defense right now, especially when the executive branch overreaches, which Trump always does.

The first line of defense is for someone to go to court and file suit in those federal district courts. We're winning, many of which you helped put together, we've won about 95% of the cases. In area after area, because we put in those judges. So it made a difference. That's why, by the way, it's so important we get the majority in 2026 in the Senate. I know people say the house is easier, it's a little easier, but the Senate, I would argue, is more important. So they can't put in these right wing judges, whether it be in the Supreme Court or in the district courts, and we can continue to have a bench that actually follows the law instead of follows Trump.

Marc: I think history will record the job you and President Biden did around judges as among the most consequential things of this era. I know that those other big bills are important, and I'm not minimizing them, but the fact is that democracy is under attack right now.

John Thune and Mike Johnson are not going to stand up against Donald Trump. The only thing we have is the judiciary, and because of the judges you helped shepherd through, and as you said, not just the sheer number, but who they are. A guy like Dale Ho, I know Dale Ho very well from his time in private practice in the ACLU, but he was someone you personally championed—

Senator Schumer: —One of the greatest voting rights lawyers in America and [Republicans] held him up because of that. But we got him through.

Marc: I give a lot of credit to your colleagues who did the Bove hearing. I thought they were great. From top to bottom, they really exposed this guy who is like Donald Trump's personal lawyer, who's his enforcer at DOJ, and I think that you guys deserve a lot of credit for the way you guys handled that.

Senator Schumer. Our judiciary committee is like — let’s put it like a Yankee’s fan – Mantle, Ruth, Gehrig, all these great baseball players, but the bottom line is that what Trump wants, what he's done, he's changed the Justice Department. It used to be one of the great things in America, the closest to a nonpartisan civil service, which followed the law and the rule of law and the Constitution, regardless of who is president.

He's turned it into an attack dog to go after his enemies and the people he doesn't like. It's incredible. And Bove is one of these people. They even say it! What did Kash Patel say? He said, “My job is to go after Trump's enemies.” That's the head of the FBI, which has been an institution, again, that followed rule of law regardless of what party was in power. And now he's made these people attack dogs to go after his enemies. It's incredible. [The Democratic senators] did a great job of exposing it.

I do believe this, Marc: when people see the price of eggs go up, when people can't afford a home, when people see their health care getting taken away, they see it immediately. We just have to show them how Trump is doing that, and as you said, we're really making good progress. Trump's numbers on the economy are now 38%. They used to be 55 because of the work we and others have done.

But it takes a little while longer for people to see that if you don't have democracy, without guardrails, it's going to allow the special interests to do all those things they don’t like.

Marc: You and I have known each other now for a long time, and I think you accurately describe me sometimes as the pessimist of the group. You are the optimist of the group. We are both Jews from New York. And I always think that what makes me the pessimist is, how could you look at 5,000 years of Jewish history and not be a pessimist? I think you came to the opposite conclusion. So why are you so optimistic?

Senator Schumer: I am, by nature, an optimist. I don't think I could do my job as well as if I wasn't an optimist.

Let me say two thoughts about that. One is something that Raphael Warnock said. We had this beautiful meeting last week. Our two Minnesota senators were so shaken by the shootings of those four people, two of which passed away in Minnesota. They were at dinner with them an hour before, and then these people went to their homes and were shot right at their doorways. It's horrible.

But at the end, I asked Raphael Warnock, who is not only a senator but a pastor, to say a prayer. And he said a line in there which sticks with me: “Evil always overreaches and thus contains in itself the seeds of its own destruction.” I believe that with Trump and in general, good prevails. Martin Luther King said, “The arc of justice is long, but bends in the direction of history.” I would add, “If we bend it.”

I'll tell you one other thing that makes me an optimist. My father. He had a tough life. He was a very smart guy, but circumstances didn't let him go to college, and didn't let him advance. He had this junky, exterminating business, which he hated. He served in it for 45 years. He died at 98 just a couple of years ago, around Thanksgiving, but he never complained that he didn't get a good break. He never resented people who might have been given a better break than him. He left me with values, and they've shaped my life.

One value: Always help people who need help. That's the most important thing you can do, and that's what our religious teachings teach us, and that's Jewish, Christian, Muslim, all of the great religions teach that. But the second thing he said is this: “If you're doing something important, look in your heart, make sure it's the right thing to do, whether it's personal or professional or family or anything, make sure it's the right thing to do. But once you're sure it's the right thing to do, persist, persist, persist. Never give up, and God will reward you and you will succeed.” That's what I do. Story of my life, persist, persist, persist. Never give up, and hopefully, even in these dark times where Trump is so evil and seems to have so much power, will persist and succeed.
